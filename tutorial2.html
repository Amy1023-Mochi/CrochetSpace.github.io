Tutorial Crochet Retro Camera Pouch ⋆.📷˚
This video tutorial shows how to crochet a retro-style camera pouch designed for beginners. It demonstrates how to work basic stitches (such as single crochet and double crochet), build the body of the pouch, shape it correctly, and assemble the final piece. The tutorial also covers finishing touches like adding details and preparing the pouch for practical use. This project is suitable for beginner crocheters who are already familiar with basic crochet stitches and want to apply their skills to create a useful and adorable accessory.


Step-by-Step Crochet Guidance


1. Prepare Your Materials

- Select a crochet hook appropriate for your yarn weight.

- Choose yarn colours you like for the camera body and details.

- Have scissors and yarn needle ready for finishing.

- If you plan to add a zipper or button, have that available too.



2. Start with a Slip Knot and Foundation Chain

- Create a slip knot on your hook.

- Chain the number of stitches needed for the width of your pouch base (as demonstrated in the video).

The chain forms the base of the pouch.



3. Work the Body with Repeated Stitches

- Turn your work after the foundation chain.

- Work single crochet (sc) or double crochet (dc) stitches across the chain.

- At the end of each row, turn your work and continue stitching to build up the height of the pouch.

Consistent tension will make the pouch even and neat.



4. Shape the Pouch

- Continue repeating rows until the body of the pouch reaches the desired height.

- The stitch type and repetition determine the pouch texture and firmness.

- Add any colour changes if required for the retro camera design.



5. Create Details

- Add features like a camera “lens,” buttons, or lines using different coloured yarn.

- These can be crocheted separately and sewn on, or crocheted directly into the pouch design.

This adds personality and makes the pouch visually resemble a camera.



6. Assemble and Finish

- Once the crochet work is complete, use a yarn needle to weave in all loose ends.

- If you are including a zipper or button closure, attach it securely so that the pouch can open and close easily.

Make sure the edges are neat and all pieces are firmly secured.
