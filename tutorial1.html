This video tutorial provides a detailed, beginner-friendly guide to basic crochet stitches. It demonstrates how to work single crochet (sc) and double crochet (dc) stitches step by step, helping new crocheters understand the mechanics and rhythm of crocheting. Through clear visual instructions, the tutorial shows how to hold the yarn and hook, create foundational stitches, and distinguish between compact and taller stitches. The video is ideal for anyone starting their crochet journey, offering practical demonstrations that can be followed at your own pace.


Step-by-Step Crochet Guidance


1. Prepare Your Materials

- Choose a crochet hook of suitable size.

- Select yarn appropriate for beginners (medium weight is recommended).

- Hold the hook comfortably in your dominant hand.



2. Make a Slip Knot

- Form a loop in the yarn.

- Insert the crochet hook into the loop.

- Pull the yarn through gently to create a slip knot on the hook.

This is the starting point for all crochet projects.



3. Create a Chain Foundation

- Yarn over (wrap the yarn over the hook).

- Pull the yarn through the loop on the hook.

- Repeat to make several chain stitches — this forms the base row.

The chain determines the width of your project.



4. Single Crochet (sc)

- Insert the hook into the second chain from the hook.

- Yarn over and pull a loop through (two loops on hook).

- Yarn over again and pull through both loops.

This completes one single crochet stitch and gives a firm, compact look.



5. Double Crochet (dc)

- Yarn over before inserting the hook into the chain.

- Insert the hook into the next chain.

- Yarn over and pull up a loop (three loops on hook).

- Yarn over and pull through two loops, then yarn over and pull through the remaining two.

Double crochet stitches are taller and create a more open texture.



6. Repeat Across the Row

- Continue making single or double crochet stitches across the foundation chain.

- At the end, turn your work to begin the next row.

This repetition builds the fabric of your crochet piece.



7. Practice and Learn Rhythm

- Practice both stitch types until you feel comfortable.

- Notice how single crochet makes a tighter fabric, and double crochet makes airier loops.

Regular practice improves consistency and tension control.
